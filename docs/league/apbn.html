APBN
Asociación Profesional de Béisbol Nicaragüense

Nicaragua is the most baseball-obsessed country in Central America — baseball outranks soccer as the national sport, a rarity anywhere outside the Caribbean basin. APBN is the country's top flight, freshly rebranded in December 2025 from the old LBPN name. Five teams compete for the national championship and the right to represent Nicaragua in the Caribbean Series. The pipeline is real: Denis Martínez (the first Nicaraguan in MLB, only pitcher to throw a perfect game while Nicaraguan), Marvin Benard, Vicente Padilla, Cheslor Cuthbert, and the current generation all came through this league first.

The Fact
Baseball Ranks Ahead of Soccer in Nicaragua — The Only Central American Country Where That's True
Walk down any street in Managua, León, or Granada and you'll see kids playing béisbol, not fútbol. Nicaragua is the only country in Central America where baseball outranks soccer as the dominant sport — a cultural inheritance from US Marines stationed in the country in the early 20th century that somehow took deeper root here than anywhere else in the region. Denis Martínez — "El Presidente" — became Nicaragua's first MLB player in 1976 and pitched a perfect game with the Expos in 1991. The Estadio Dennis Martínez National Stadium in Managua holds 15,000 and regularly sells out for Serie Final games. Nicaragua hosted the Serie de las Américas as recently as 2024 in Managua, drew international television coverage, and beat both Colombia and Panama on national TV. The rebrand from LBPN to APBN in December 2025 signals the federation's renewed international ambitions. If you want to watch the most under-appreciated baseball culture in the hemisphere, this is where it lives.
