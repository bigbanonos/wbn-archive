LAMP
Liga ARCO Mexicana del Pacífico

Mexico's winter league on the Pacific coast — complementing LMB's summer season in the interior. Ten teams across Sonora, Sinaloa, Sonora, Jalisco, and the Baja peninsula. Famously free on YouTube — every game, no subscription, global access. Charros de Jalisco are defending 2025–26 champions. The league winner represents Mexico in the February Caribbean Series, where Mexican teams have won 11 championships — more than any country except the Dominican Republic.

⚡ When to Watch LAMP from the US
Most LAMP teams play on Mountain Time (UTC-7) — 2 hours behind US Eastern. A 19:30 MST first pitch = 9:30 PM ET. Weekend day games at 16:00 MST = 6:00 PM ET. The Jalisco-based teams (Charros, Mariachis) play Central Time, making them even closer to East Coast clocks.

The Fact
LAMP is Mexico's Caribbean Series Engine
Mexico has won 11 Caribbean Series championships — more than any country except the Dominican Republic (20 titles). And almost every one of those 11 titles came from a LAMP champion, not a summer-league LMB team. The Naranjeros de Hermosillo and Tomateros de Culiacán have combined for most of Mexico's Caribbean Series success. The reason: LAMP plays on Mexican Pacific time, in Mexican Pacific climate, against the fiercest competition in Mexican baseball. By February, when the Caribbean Series starts, LAMP's champion has already played 68 regular-season games plus best-of-7 playoff rounds against nine other teams. They are battle-tested in a way no Caribbean winter team is. And because every LAMP game streams free on YouTube, US fans can watch that battle all winter long — four months of high-level baseball at 9:30 PM ET, every night, at no cost.
