LBPRC
Liga de Béisbol Profesional Roberto Clemente

Puerto Rico's winter league, named in 2012 for the only player in modern baseball who has a country's league named after him. Six historic franchises, 88 years of history, and every game streams free on YouTube. Santurce Cangrejeros are the defending champions — the franchise that once featured Willie Mays, Roberto Clemente, and Josh Gibson on the same roster. LBPRC sent Puerto Rico to two WBC finals (2013, 2017) and produces the Molina brothers, Yadier, Bengie, and José, plus Roberto Alomar, Iván Rodríguez, and Francisco Lindor.

The Fact
Named for Clemente — And He Deserves Every Syllable
The league was renamed in 2012 to the Liga de Béisbol Profesional Roberto Clemente, forty years after the Pirates right fielder died in a plane crash on December 31, 1972, flying relief supplies to earthquake victims in Nicaragua. He played winter ball for Santurce Cangrejeros every single offseason of his professional career — he considered it non-negotiable. On January 11, 1954, Clemente batted seventh and started in right field for Santurce in a game where Willie Mays batted third and played center, Josh Gibson's Homestead Grays tradition lived in the batboy room, and the Cangrejeros manager fielded what may be the greatest outfield ever assembled anywhere. Clemente's Puerto Rican identity was the core of everything. The LBPRC preserves that. Every LBPRC game on YouTube is free because the league prioritizes access over profit — exactly what Clemente himself would have wanted. The YouTube handle, @lbprc21tv, ends in 21. That's his number. That will never change.
