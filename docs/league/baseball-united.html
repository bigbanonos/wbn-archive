BASEBALL UNITED
Professional League · UAE · India · Pakistan · Saudi Arabia

The first professional baseball league for the Middle East and South Asia. Six clubs. Dubai as the hub. Founded and backed by Mariano Rivera, Barry Larkin, Manny Ramirez, and investor-commissioner Kash Shaikh. The business thesis: 1.8 billion people in a region with zero pro baseball before 2024. Target markets are India, Pakistan, UAE, Saudi Arabia. The Mid-Season Showcase runs November through February. The Fact
The World's Largest Untapped Baseball Market — And Nobody's There Yet
Baseball United is the first professional baseball league ever founded in the region that includes India (population 1.4B), Pakistan (240M), UAE (10M), and Saudi Arabia (36M). That's a combined 1.8 billion potential viewers with zero pro baseball history. The league's thesis is simple: you don't compete for existing baseball fans, you build new ones. Cricket supplies the cultural context — pitching, batting, running, fielding translate. The Dubai hub is deliberate: hub airport, crypto-friendly banking, low taxes, existing sports infrastructure from the Indian Premier League and F1. When Kash Shaikh assembled Rivera, Larkin, and Manny as advisors, he wasn't recruiting for name recognition — he was recruiting the only three humans who could walk into a Riyadh or Mumbai pitch meeting and be the most respected person in the room.
