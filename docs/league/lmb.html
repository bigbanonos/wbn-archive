LMB
Liga Mexicana de Béisbol Banorte

Mexico's summer league is the oldest continuously-operating professional sports league in the country — founded 1925, now in its 101st season. Twenty teams play a 93-game schedule in brutal Mexican summer heat. The Diablos Rojos del México are chasing a third consecutive championship. The Charros de Jalisco play in the baseball capital no American talks about.

⚡ When to Watch LMB from the US
LMB is perfectly timed for US East Coast viewers. Weekday games at 19:30 Mexico City time = 8:30 PM ET — right on the MLB prime-time schedule. Weekend games at 16:00 CST = 5:00 PM ET. Teams in Jalisco and Chihuahua play on Mountain time, which adds an extra hour.

The Fact
Guadalajara Is the Baseball Capital Nobody Talks About
Matt Tallarini (WBN) and Leif Skodnick have both reported from Guadalajara, and both came back with the same observation: nothing in American baseball prepares you for it. The city is more famous worldwide for tequila and mariachi, but the ballpark culture is electric — every run celebrated like a playoff walk-off. The Charros de Jalisco play at Estadio Panamericano in altitude and heat, among fans who treat losing like personal insult. The LMB season runs through August in the Mexican summer, which means on-field conditions are brutal — 95°F+ with humidity, 93 games in 110 days. American pitchers who come to LMB to extend careers talk about it like a different sport. It is.
